# HCI glossary

Terms to keep precise vs. terms to treat as filler in this domain.

## Keep precise (use when accurate)
- affordance, signifier (Gibson; Norman)
- tangible interaction, embodied interaction, graspable UI
- material, materiality, material qualities, material probes
- research-through-design (RtD), annotated portfolio, pictorial
- strong concept, intermediate-level knowledge, design exemplar
- making, craft, prototyping, sketching in hardware
- felt experience, somaesthetic, lived experience
- assemblage, entanglement, more-than-human, posthuman (when grounded)
- speculative / critical / discursive design (when grounded)

## Treat as AI filler (replace or cut)
- seamless, intuitive, user-centric, user-friendly, holistic
- novel framework, transformative, groundbreaking, paradigm shift
- bridges the gap, at the intersection of, in today's landscape
- leverage, robust, scalable, cutting-edge
- "empowers users to…", "unlocks the potential of…"
- generic "engagement", "experience" without a referent

## Use carefully (often misused by AI)
- "design space" — fine when you actually map one; not a synonym for "topic"
- "implications for design" — only if you actually derive them from the artifact
- "co-design", "participatory" — only if participants actually shaped decisions
